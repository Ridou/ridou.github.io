Stereotype

Diversity threatens stereotypes

I was raised in Puerto Rico which is the root of the melting pot in America. The diversity with my peers was pretty broad and because of this I learned of different cultures and religions. With diversity one can see the stereotypes being reduced and people just to who they really are. I did experience a social status stereotype where some of my friends would treat others negatively because they came from a lower social status and would refrain from being close to them. I did not like this, so I would hear what they had to say and then hang out with that person at another time because I also wanted to know their point of view on the other social group.

I frequently asked myself why the idea of their family having more money mean they cannot be friends. I would tell them your family is rich, but you have no capital, so we are all on the same page. I tried relating with all the groups to better understand different types of persona, so I was malleable to all my peers.

There was one time in a history class that I will never forget and it is a bit insulting. A professor of mine was talking about slaves brought to Puerto Rico and he said that they would discriminate by the physical attributes of the person and when he said curly hair he pointed at me and called me a "negro" in a mischievous way. Everyone laughed and I bit my tongue to tell him, so what does it matter? you are a gay professor in a catholic school, isn't that an oxymoron? I was tempted to say this but putting myself to that level would not bring anything positive to the situation. I would just stare him down every time we would meet, because he knew that I thought lowly of himself.
-Richard Santin
