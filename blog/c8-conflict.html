A Conflicting Story

Compass of Shame

When I was a teenager I was late for many friend meetings because it was typical to be late in the island culture. There was no conflict when someone would arrive late so, people tended to just be late. Of course for school, business and special occasions people would arrive on time.

The conflict arose when I went to Japan and I experienced a bit of culture shock because everyone there was always 5 to 15 minutes early. Some of my friends would try to hide but would be disappointed it if I was even 5 minutes late. When I was late I would apologize to them to be late and although that would make the conflict better it did not fix the way they thought of me. It kinda led to them not trusting me the next time of being on time and that trust could lead to other things.

I have currently changed to a more punctual person and also arriving early to the meeting let it be with a friend or for business. Now that this is my way of life I do not like making people wait for me, but I also have patience for waiting for people that are late because I was raised in a culture was always late for social meetings.
-Richard Santin
