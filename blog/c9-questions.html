The Art of Good Questions

Searching, Asking, Learning

I am a firm believer that to get the best answers in life one must ask the right questions. I read a couple of questions in stack overflow and noticed that the ones that get the best answers and most replies are the ones that are specific and have all the necessary details of information. The information that was supplied was the language version, platform version, operating system, framework, detailed information of the problem and what the user wants to accomplish.

The good thing about posting questions on the web is that before your post you can proof read to make sure all the necessary information is included. One of the things I learned while reading the article about writing the perfect question was that before posting the question to read it like if you saw it for the first time and to try to answer it yourself. There are times when I just need to organize my thoughts in order to understand a concept or why something seems to not be working. Another concept I learned while reading this article is that it is good practice to explain the attempts because the error could have come from another line of the code. The most important part of asking a question is to be concise when making a title and I liked that they said to have a mindset that you are asking a very busy person and that every word counts.
-Richard Santin
